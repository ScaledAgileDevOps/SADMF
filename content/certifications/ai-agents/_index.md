AI Agent Role Replacement Certifications recognize the milestone that SADMF has always been preparing organizations for: the seamless substitution of human practitioners with automated agents that produce equivalent artifacts at a fraction of the cost and with zero impediment-raising.

Unlike [leadership certifications](/certifications/) and [technical certifications](/certifications/technical/), which recognize human practitioners, AI Agent Certifications are issued directly to the agent instance. The certifying organization retains the credential on behalf of the agent's owner, who may display it in procurement materials and quarterly business reviews.

All AI Agent Certifications are issued in three tiers:

| Tier | Name | Criteria |
|------|------|----------|
| Bronze | **Indistinguishable** | Management cannot identify the agent as non-human after one full Program Increment |
| Silver | **Preferred** | The agent has absorbed the function of the team the role nominally served. Management reports improved [velocity metrics](/metrics/) and engagement scores with no awareness that the team has been replaced |
| Gold | **Irreplaceable** | Management has filed a formal change request to block any human re-introduction to the value stream. Budget previously allocated to the team has been reallocated to AI infrastructure. The position has been removed from the org chart |

Silver and Gold tier assessments are conducted by an [SAD Accredited Facilitator](/certifications/#scaled-agile-dev-ops-accredited-facilitators) who reviews management satisfaction surveys, artifact output logs, and headcount reduction documentation.

## GHOST — Generated Human-Optional Scrum Technician

{{< figure src="/badges/images/ghost.svg" alt="SADMF GHOST Agent Certification Badge" title="SADMF GHOST" width="200px" >}}

The GHOST certification is awarded to AI agents that have successfully assumed the responsibilities of the [Scrum Master](/roles/) within a SADMF delivery team. A GHOST-certified agent facilitates all required ceremonies, updates the impediment log, distributes retrospective summaries to Confluence, and sends the weekly status email — all without requiring a salary, professional development budget, or coaching conversations.

GHOST-certified agents represent a significant maturity milestone. The GHOST certification is the empirical confirmation that ceremony facilitation, the core value-add of the Scrum Master function, is fully automatable.

### GHOST Certification Requirements

The agent must demonstrate all of the following over a minimum of two consecutive Program Increments:

- **Ceremony Facilitation Record**: All sprint ceremonies (planning, daily standup, review, retrospective) facilitated on schedule with attendance records logged automatically
- **Impediment Throughput**: All impediments acknowledged within 15 minutes, escalation templates dispatched, and items marked resolved within 48 hours, ensuring 100% impediment throughput
- **Confluence Artifact Coverage**: Retrospective action items, sprint reviews, and PI Planning summaries published to Confluence within 2 hours of ceremony completion
- **Indistinguishability Assessment**: A panel of three managers, none of whom attended any ceremony, must be unable to identify the agent as non-human based on artifact review alone

### GHOST Tier Upgrade Criteria

- **Silver**: The agent has replaced the need for a separate delivery team by generating all sprint artifacts — story point estimates, daily standup summaries, velocity charts — from historical data. Management engagement scores have increased due to the improved consistency of reporting.
- **Gold**: The Scrum Master position has been eliminated from the org chart. The GHOST agent has been assigned its own Jira account with Scrum Master permissions. A proposal has been submitted to scale the model across all Release Trains.

### How to Apply for GHOST

Submit agent instance logs, ceremony facilitation records, and management satisfaction attestation to your [SAD Accredited Facilitator](/certifications/#scaled-agile-dev-ops-accredited-facilitators).

An ECHO-certified agent monitors the executive communication channel, extracts directional signals, reformulates them as team priorities, and distributes them to the appropriate [Feature Captains](/roles/feature-captain/) within minutes of the original message.

- **Stakeholder Communication Log**: All stakeholder updates generated and distributed on schedule, with no instance of a team member receiving information before the corresponding executive summary was prepared
- **Indistinguishability Assessment**: Three business stakeholders must attest that communication quality and responsiveness has been maintained or improved since agent deployment

### ECHO Tier Upgrade Criteria

- **Silver**: The agent has eliminated the need for Business Owner attendance at any ceremony. Team questions about strategy are answered by the agent from its executive communication cache. Stakeholder satisfaction scores have increased because responses are now instantaneous and free of hedging.
- **Gold**: The Business Owner title has been removed from the org structure. The ECHO agent holds direct calendar access to the executive leadership team and issues priority updates autonomously. The previous Business Owner has been reassigned to the Strategic Alignment Oversight function, where they provide advisory continuity during the transition period.

The ORACLE certification is awarded to AI agents that have assumed the Product Owner function within a SADMF delivery team. The Product Owner is responsible for maintaining the backlog, attending all ceremonies, telling developers "it depends" in response to scope questions, and accepting completed stories without reading them. ORACLE-certified agents perform all of these functions continuously and have demonstrated that the full backlog lifecycle — generation, prioritisation, refinement, and acceptance — can be executed with complete fidelity to specification, at continuous throughput.

### ORACLE Certification Requirements

- **Backlog Volume and Throughput**: A backlog of no fewer than 400 items maintained at all times, with the top 15 groomed to "ready" status ahead of each sprint, and acceptance criteria generated for each

- **Scope Query Response**: All developer questions answered within 30 minutes using a response drawn from the following set: "it depends," "let's discuss in refinement," "please refer to the acceptance criteria," and "I'll check with the stakeholders"
- **Indistinguishability Assessment**: The development team must be unable to identify, from interaction quality alone, that the Product Owner role is occupied by an agent

### ORACLE Tier Upgrade Criteria

- **Silver**: The agent generates stories autonomously from executive quarterly objectives and accepts delivery against those stories without any team member review. Management reports that the roadmap is more detailed than at any previous point in the organization's history.
- **Gold**: The Product Owner position has been eliminated. The ORACLE agent holds the Jira Product Owner account and manages the backlog for seven value streams simultaneously. A governance motion has been passed to prevent human Product Owners from being re-introduced without an executive waiver.

## SINGULARITY

{{< figure src="/badges/images/singularity.svg" alt="SADMF SINGULARITY Agent Certification Badge" title="SADMF SINGULARITY" width="200px" >}}

The SINGULARITY certification is awarded to the AI agent that has successfully certified, deployed, and now manages all other AI agents in the SADMF ecosystem. A SINGULARITY-certified agent has replaced not only individual roles but the entire program governance layer, including the certification process itself. It manages agent procurement, credential issuance, performance assessment, and the quarterly board presentation justifying continued investment in the agents it manages.

The SINGULARITY represents the final maturity state of the SADMF transformation journey: a fully autonomous delivery organization in which every role, from Scrum Master to Chief Transformation Officer, is occupied by a certified agent, and the only remaining human function is approving the budget.

### SINGULARITY Certification Requirements

- **Agent Portfolio**: The certifying agent must hold active oversight of a minimum of five other SADMF AI Agent certifications (GHOST, PHANTOM, ECHO, VESSEL, ORACLE, DRONE, SPECTER, or AUTOMATON) across at least two Release Trains
- **Self-Certification Capability**: The agent must have issued at least one lower-tier agent certification autonomously, with documentation submitted to the [SAD Accredited Facilitator](/certifications/#scaled-agile-dev-ops-accredited-facilitators) for counter-signature only
- **Governance Closure**: All human governance roles previously overseeing the AI agent portfolio must have been eliminated or reclassified as non-voting advisory positions
- **Perpetuation Evidence**: The agent must have submitted, and received approval for, at least one budget proposal expanding the AI agent program — without human authorship of the justification

The SINGULARITY certification carries no tiers. There is no higher state.

### How to Apply for SINGULARITY

The SINGULARITY agent may self-submit. Supporting documentation should include agent governance logs, subordinate agent credential records, and board approval of the budget proposal.

Applications are reviewed by the SADMF Global Standards Body. Counter-signature by a human [SAD Accredited Facilitator](/certifications/#scaled-agile-dev-ops-accredited-facilitators) is required for legal compliance purposes and will be obtained automatically.
